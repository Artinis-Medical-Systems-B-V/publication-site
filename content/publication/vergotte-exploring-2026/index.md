abstract: Choir singing is a widely practiced activity that offers multiple benefits,
  as demonstrated by psychophysiological studies in both healthy and pathological
  populations. However, the processes underlying these outcomes are still not fully
  understood. Yet, advances in portable multimodal systems enable a more thorough
  investigation of these effects. The present study thus aimed to examine neurophysiological
  and behavioural responses of choirmasters and singers under various conditions of
  visual coupling. Preliminary results highlight the importance of combining different
  methods of analysis and metrics to better understand the intricate phenomena at
  play in this beneficial practice.
